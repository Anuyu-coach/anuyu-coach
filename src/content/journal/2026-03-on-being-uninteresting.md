When I started coaching, I worried I wasn't interesting enough. I thought the
work was about saying the cleverest possible thing at the cleverest possible
time. That if a client left without a quote-worthy takeaway, I had failed
them.

It took me about a year to realize the opposite is true.

## The room before the room

The most useful thing I can offer most weeks is not a sharp question. It's
a slower room. A room where the client can stop performing — for me, for
themselves, for whoever they were going to call after.

> The work is not to become someone new. It is to stop rehearsing someone
> you're not.

That sentence has been on the wall of the studio for two years now. I wrote
it on a sticky note in 2024 and never moved it.

## What I'm trying

This season I'm experimenting with starting every session in silence for
sixty seconds. Just sitting. Not as a meditation — as a marker. A way to
say: *we are no longer in the meeting before this one.*

It feels small. Most things that matter do.

— A.
